     70 &ndash; Siege of Jerusalem: Titus and his Roman legions breach the Second Wall of Jerusalem. Jewish defenders retreat to the First Wall. The Roman Catholic Church remembers this day as the celebration of Saint Joan of Arc. 1434 &ndash; Hussite Wars: Battle of Lipany: Effectively ending the war, Utraquist forces led by Diviš Bořek of Miletínek defeat and almost annihilate Taborite forces led by Prokop the Great. 1510 &ndash; During the reign of the Zhengde Emperor, Ming dynasty rebel leader Zhu Zhifan is defeated by commander Qiu Yue, ending the Prince of Anhua rebellion. 1536 &ndash; King Henry VIII of England marries Jane Seymour, a lady-in-waiting to his first two wives. 1539 &ndash; In Florida, Hernando de Soto lands at Tampa Bay with 600 soldiers with the goal of finding gold. 1574 &ndash; Henry III becomes King of France. 1588 &ndash; The last ship of the Spanish Armada sets sail from Lisbon heading for the English Channel. 1631 &ndash; Publication of Gazette de France, the first French newspaper. 1635 &ndash; Thirty Years' War: The Peace of Prague is signed. 1642 &ndash; From this date all honors granted by Charles I are retrospectively annulled by Parliament. 1806 &ndash; Future U.S. President Andrew Jackson kills Charles Dickinson in a duel after Dickinson had accused Jackson's wife of bigamy. 1814 &ndash; Napoleonic Wars: War of the Sixth Coalition: The Treaty of Paris (1814) is signed returning French borders to their 1792 extent. Napoleon is exiled to Elba. 1815 &ndash; The East Indiaman Arniston is wrecked during a storm at Waenhuiskrans, near Cape Agulhas, in present-day South Africa, with the loss of 372 lives. 1832 &ndash; End of the Hambach Festival in Rhineland-Palatinate, Germany. 1832   &ndash; The Rideau Canal in eastern Ontario is opened. 1834 &ndash; Joaquim António de Aguiar issues a law extinguishing "all convents, monasteries, colleges, hospices and any other houses of the regular religious orders" in Portugal, earning him the nickname of "The Friar-Killer". 1842 &ndash; John Francis attempts to murder Queen Victoria as she drives down Constitution Hill in London with Prince Albert. 1845 &ndash; The Fatel Razack lands in the Gulf of Paria in Trinidad and Tobago carrying the first East Indians to the country. 1854 &ndash; The Kansas–Nebraska Act becomes law establishing the US territories of Kansas and Nebraska. 1868 &ndash; Decoration Day (the predecessor of the modern "Memorial Day") is observed in the United States for the first time (by "Commander-in-chief of the Grand Army of the Republic" John A. Albania becomes an independent nation. 1914 &ndash; The new, and then the largest, Cunard ocean liner , 45,647 tons, sets sails on her maiden voyage from Liverpool, England, to New York City. 1917 &ndash; Alexander I becomes king of Greece. 1922 &ndash; The Lincoln Memorial is dedicated in Washington, D.C.. 1925 &ndash; May Thirtieth Movement: Shanghai Municipal Police Force shoot and kill 13 protesting workers. 1932 &ndash; The National Theatre of Greece is founded. 1937 &ndash; Memorial Day massacre: Chicago police shoot and kill ten labor demonstrators. 1941 &ndash; World War II: Manolis Glezos and Apostolos Santas climb the Athenian Acropolis and tear down the Nazi swastika. 1942 &ndash; World War II: One thousand British bombers launch a 90-minute attack on Cologne, Germany. 1948 &ndash; A dike along the flooding Columbia River breaks, obliterating Vanport, Oregon, within minutes. Immediately after, less than one million of his supporters march on the Champs-Élysées in Paris.
