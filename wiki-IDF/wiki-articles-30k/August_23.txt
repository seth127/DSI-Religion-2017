       30 BC &ndash; After the successful invasion of Egypt, Octavian executes Marcus Antonius Antyllus, eldest son of Mark Antony, and Caesarion, the last king of the Ptolemaic dynasty of Egypt and only child of Julius Caesar and Cleopatra. 20 BC &ndash; Ludi Volcanalici are held within the temple precinct of Vulcan, and used by Augustus to mark the treaty with Parthia and the return of the legionary standards that had been lost at the Battle of Carrhae in 53 BC. 79 &ndash; Mount Vesuvius begins stirring, on the feast day of Vulcan, the Roman god of fire. 406 &ndash; Gothic king Radagaisus is executed after he is defeated by Roman general Stilicho and 12,000 "barbarians" are incorporated into the Roman army or sold as slaves. 476 &ndash; Odoacer, chieftain of the Germanic tribes (Herulic - Scirian foederati), is proclaimed rex Italiae ("King of Italy") by his troops. 634 &ndash; Abu Bakr dies at Medina and is succeeded by Umar I who becomes the second caliph of the Rashidun Caliphate. 1244 &ndash; Siege of Jerusalem: The city's citadel, the Tower of David, surrenders to Khwarezmian Empire. 1268 &ndash; Battle of Tagliacozzo: The army of Charles of Anjou defeats the Ghibellines supporters of Conradin of Hohenstaufen marking the fall of the Hohenstaufen family from the Imperial and Sicilian thrones, and leading to the new chapter of Angevin domination in Southern Italy. 1305 &ndash; Sir William Wallace is executed for high treason at Smithfield in London. 1328 &ndash; Battle of Cassel: French troops stop an uprising of Flemish farmers. 1382 &ndash; Siege of Moscow: The Golden Horde led by khan Tokhtamysh lays siege to the capital of the Grand Duchy of Moscow. 1514 &ndash; The Battle of Chaldiran ends with a decisive victory for the Sultan Selim I, Ottoman Empire, over the Shah Ismail I, founder of the Safavid dynasty. 1521 &ndash; Christian II of Denmark is deposed as king of Sweden and Gustav Vasa is elected regent. 1541 &ndash; French explorer Jacques Cartier lands near Quebec City in his third voyage to Canada. 1572 &ndash; French Wars of Religion Mob violence against Huguenots in Paris results in the St. Bartholomew's Day massacre. 1592 &ndash; Japanese invasions of Korea: The Yeongwon Castle is besieged by the Japanese Fourth Division led by Itō Suketaka. 1595 &ndash; Long Turkish War: Wallachian prince Michael the Brave confronts the Ottoman army in the Battle of Călugăreni and achieves a tactical victory. 1600 &ndash; Battle of Gifu Castle: The eastern forces of Tokugawa Ieyasu defeat the western Japanese clans loyal to Toyotomi Hideyori, leading to the destruction of Gifu Castle and serving as a prelude to the Battle of Sekigahara. 1614 &ndash; Fettmilch Uprising: Jews are expelled from Frankfurt, Holy Roman Empire, following the plundering of the Judengasse. 1614   &ndash; The University of Groningen is established in the Dutch Republic. 1628 &ndash; George Villiers, the first Duke of Buckingham, is assassinated by John Felton. 1650 &ndash; Colonel George Monck of the English Army forms Monck's Regiment of Foot, which will later become the Coldstream Guards. 1655 &ndash; Battle of Sobota: The Swedish Empire led by Charles X Gustav defeats the Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth. 1703 &ndash; Edirne event: Sultan Mustafa II of the Ottoman Empire is dethroned. 1765 &ndash; Beginning of Burmese–Siamese War. 1775 &ndash; American Revolutionary War: King George III delivers his Proclamation of Rebellion to the Court of St James's stating that the American colonies have proceeded to a state of open and avowed rebellion. 1784 &ndash; Western North Carolina (now eastern Tennessee) declares itself an independent state under the name of Franklin; it is not accepted into the United States, and only lasts for four years. 1799 &ndash; Napoleon I of France leaves Egypt for France en route to seizing power. 1813 &ndash; At the Battle of Großbeeren, the Prussians under Von Bülow repulse the French army. 1831 &ndash; Nat Turner's slave rebellion is suppressed. 1839 &ndash; The United Kingdom captures Hong Kong as a base as it prepares for war with Qing China.  The ensuing 3-year conflict will later be known as the First Opium War. 1858 &ndash; The Round Oak rail accident occurs in Brierley Hill in the Black Country, England. It is 'Arguably the worst disaster ever to occur on British railways'. 1864 &ndash; The Union Navy captures Fort Morgan, Alabama, thus breaking Confederate dominance of all ports on the Gulf of Mexico except Galveston, Texas. 1866 &ndash; Austro-Prussian War ends with the Treaty of Prague. 1873 &ndash; Albert Bridge in Chelsea, London opens. 1896 &ndash; Officially recognised date of the Cry of Pugad Lawin, the start of the Philippine Revolution is made in Pugad Lawin (Quezon City), in the province of Manila (actual date and location is disputed). 1898 &ndash; The Southern Cross Expedition, the first British venture of the Heroic Age of Antarctic Exploration, departs from London. 1901 &ndash; 600 American school teachers, Thomasites, arrived in Manila on the USAT Thomas. 1904 &ndash; The automobile tire chain is patented. 1914 &ndash; World War I: Japan declares war on Germany and bombs Qingdao, China. 1914   &ndash; World War I: Battle of Mons: The British Army begins withdrawal. 1921 &ndash; British airship R-38 experiences structural failure over Hull in England and crashes in the Humber estuary. Of her 49 British and American training crew, only four survive. 1923 &ndash; Captain Lowell Smith and Lieutenant John P. Richter performed the first mid-air refueling on De Havilland DH-4B, setting an endurance flight record of 37 hours. 1927 &ndash; Italian anarchists Sacco and Vanzetti are executed after a lengthy, controversial trial. 1929 &ndash; Hebron Massacre during the 1929 Palestine riots: Arab attack on the Jewish community in Hebron in the British Mandate of Palestine, continuing until the next day, resulted in the death of 65–68 Jews and the remaining Jews being forced to leave the city. 1938 &ndash; English cricketer Len Hutton sets a world record for the highest individual Test innings of 364, during a Test match against  Australia. 1939 &ndash; World War II: Nazi Germany and the Soviet Union sign a non-aggression treaty, the Molotov–Ribbentrop Pact. In a secret addition to the pact, the Baltic states, Finland, Romania, and Poland are divided between the two nations. 1942 &ndash; World War II: Beginning of the Battle of Stalingrad. 1943 &ndash; World War II: Kharkov is liberated as a result of the Battle of Kursk. 1944 &ndash; World War II: Marseille is liberated by the Allies. 1944   &ndash; World War II: King Michael of Romania dismisses the pro-Nazi government of Marshal Antonescu, who is arrested.
