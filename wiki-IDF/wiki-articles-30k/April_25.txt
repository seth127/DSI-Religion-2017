      404 BC &ndash; Peloponnesian War: Lysander's Spartan armies defeated the Athenians and the war ends. 775 &ndash; The Battle of Bagrevand puts an end to an Armenian rebellion against the Abbasid Caliphate. Pelletier becomes the first person executed by guillotine. 1792   &ndash; "La Marseillaise" (the French national anthem) is composed by Claude Joseph Rouget de Lisle. 1804 &ndash; The western Georgian kingdom of Imereti accepts the suzerainty of the Russian Empire 1829 &ndash; U.S. Supreme Court delivers its opinion in Erie Railroad Co. v. Tompkins and overturns a century of federal common law. 1940 &ndash; Merkið, the flag of the Faroe Islands is approved by the British occupation government. 1943 &ndash; The Demyansk Shield for German troops in commemoration of Demyansk Pocket is instituted. 1944 &ndash; The United Negro College Fund is incorporated. 1945 &ndash; Elbe Day: United States and Soviet troops meet in Torgau along the River Elbe, cutting the Wehrmacht of Nazi Germany in two, a milestone in the approaching end of World War II in Europe. 1945   &ndash; Liberation Day (Italy): The Nazi occupation army surrenders and leaves Northern Italy after a general partisan insurrection by the Italian resistance movement; the puppet fascist regime dissolves and Benito Mussolini is captured after trying to escape.
